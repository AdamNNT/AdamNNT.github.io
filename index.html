The Future of a UX Designer

Hypothesis

As we get deeper into the Internet of Things era many occupations are having to have to adapt to the rapid change. These occupations having to adapt include many different forms of engineers to various types of designers, more specifically the UX designer or user experience designer. The UX designer stands to be heavily impacted with the Internet of Things and it’s growing number of smart connected objects now being able to connect to the internet.

Background

What is the IoT, a.k.a. the Internet of Things? According to the article “How Smart, Connected Products Are Transforming Competition” by Michael E. Porter, the phrase “internet of things” reflects the growing number of smart, connected products and highlight the new opportunities they can represent. In this article porter goes into greater detail explaining how the Internet of Things is transforming they way things are being done in businesses, as well as the effects it has on competition.

The second article “Enabling the Internet of Things” by Roy Want, Bill Schilit, and Scott Jenson analyze several aspects of the Internet of Things including the definition, and what it means for the future of certain software. This article also goes into it depth whether or not the Internet of things exist, concluding that it does exist but only for those businesses who are affected by it currently.

The third reference is a documentary by PBS called the “Big Data Revolution” where through out the video they explain and provide various examples of big data gathering. It is reinforced in this documentary that big data has big plans for the future, proposing new developments and transformations regarding our interaction with our everyday technology, effectively changing the way we live.

The fourth reference document I will mention is actually a slideshow about the big data & analytics. This slideshow examines big data itself which can be interpreted as “a collection of data sets so large and complex that it becomes difficult to process using on-hand database management tools. Through out this slideshow presentation the examination of the trend of big data and how it used to prevent diseases, spot business trends, combat crime, etc. It then goes into depth on big data’s 3V’s that include: volume, velocity, and variety. Volume meaning scale or the volume of the data, velocity regarding speed of the data being gathered, and variety referring to complexity of the data. This slideshow concludes by explaining the type of technology and services that use big data.

Discussion

"Information is now a design material." - Mike Kuniavsky

Now as I stated above the Internet of Things is will have a big impact on the UX designer. How and why will the Internet of Things have such an impact on UX designers? You ask. The internet of Things as been affecting UX designers for the last 10-15 years. If you think back to the user experience that began on our desktop, overtime changing with the constant evolution of smartphones, tablets, watches, and televisions. UX designers are already being effected, but now we are taking another step forward that may have big implications for how UX designers will design our interaction with future devices. Due to more devices being “connected” this is calling for innovative ways to create a good user experience with less and less GUI. More devices will begin to have small or no visual interfaces, which results in fewer buttons. We are entering time period where we will most likely interact with devices with our voice, or some form of gestures. Personally I think the challenge of creating a user experience on one device that can be carried over through other devices will probably be one of the bigger challenges faced by UX designers moving forward. Since so many devices in the future will be connected, most likely users will eventually want a seamless interaction between all of their devices. This an example of seamless user experience can be described when a user wants to capture a screenshot from his tv, then transfer it to his phone, while simultaneously transferring the screenshot to his laptop, all done through voice or gestures. I know, I know sound like a scene out of the movie Iron Man, while our devices of the may not be be able to do everything Mr.Stark’s did in the movie we can expect a similar user experience regarding the interaction with voice and gestures. A main goal by Alan Kay to achieve moving forward is to make it so the computer disappears into the environemt.

Conclusion

To conclude the Internet of Things will have a bigger impact on our everyday lives. Due more devices being developed to connect to the internet and each other, this will result in companies gathering even more big data. With this trend continuing our lives and everyday interaction with the devices we use will become more and more tailored specific to the individual. I believe a gave enough evidence to support my hypothesis proving UX designers will have big challenges and big opportunities ahead of them. UX designers will be essentially designing our interaction, and most importantly our user experience.
